Cricket

Overview: Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players. It originated in England and is extremely popular in India.
Rules: Runs are scored by hitting the ball and running between wickets. A team is all out when ten players are dismissed.
Equipment: Bat, cricket ball, pads, gloves, helmet, stumps.
Major Tournaments: ICC World Cup, T20 World Cup, Indian Premier League.
Health Benefits: Improves stamina, reflexes, hand-eye coordination.

Football

Overview: Football is a team sport played by kicking a ball into the opponent‚Äôs goal.
Rules: Each team has 11 players. Hands cannot be used except by the goalkeeper.
Equipment: Football, boots, shin guards, goalpost.
Major Tournaments: FIFA World Cup, UEFA Champions League.
Health Benefits: Builds endurance, strength and teamwork.

Basketball

Overview: Basketball is a fast-paced indoor game where points are scored by shooting the ball through a hoop.
Equipment: Basketball, hoop, backboard.
Major League: NBA (National Basketball Association).

Badminton

Overview: Badminton is a racket sport played with a shuttlecock across a net.
Health Benefits: Improves agility, reflexes and stamina.

Hockey

Overview: Field hockey is a team sport played with sticks and a ball.
Major Achievements: India has won multiple Olympic gold medals.

Kabaddi

Overview: Kabaddi is a traditional Indian sport requiring strength, strategy and stamina.
League: Pro Kabaddi League.

Tennis

Overview: Tennis is played using rackets on a rectangular court divided by a net.
Grand Slams: Wimbledon, US Open, French Open, Australian Open.

Athletics

Overview: Athletics includes running, jumping and throwing events.
Events: Sprint, marathon, long jump, javelin throw.
